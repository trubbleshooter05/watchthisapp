## PHASE 3: GENERATE 5 LONG-FORM ARTICLES (1,200+ WORDS EACH)

**Task:** Draft 5 articles using advanced film theory terminology and rigorous argumentation.

### Article Generation Requirements:

**Structure (Non-Negotiable):**
```
1. YAML Frontmatter (40-50 chars description, unique slug)
2. Opening Hook (1-2 paragraphs): Establish thesis + cinematic urgency
3. Body Sections (3-5 sections with H2 headers)
   - Each section: 200-300 words minimum
   - Subsections with H3 headers (if needed)
   - Theory + film examples in every section
4. Concluding Section: Synthesis + implications for contemporary cinema
5. Word Count: 1,200-1,600 words minimum
```

**Film Theory Terminology Requirements:**

You MUST use advanced critical vocabulary in every article. Examples (use liberally):
- Diegesis/Diegetic/Non-diegetic
- Mise-en-scène/Mise-en-shot
- Focal length semantics
- Temporal montage / Eisenstein principles
- Indexicality (Peirce) / Photographic indexicality
- Focalization (Genette)
- Verisimilitude vs. diegetic plausibility
- Haptic visuality (Marks)
- Suture (Oudart/Miller)
- Ideological apparatus (Althusser/Baudry)
- Apparatus theory
- Dispositif (Foucault)
- Spectatorial position / Visual pleasure (Mulvey)
- Profilmic reality (Bazin)
- Spatial grammar / Cognitive mapping
- Decoupage / Shot scale semantics
- Continuity editing vs. discontinuity
- Sound design: acousmatic/acousmatique (Chion)
- Color theory: chromatic symbolism
- Lighting as narrative device
- Depth of field as meaning-making
- Aspect ratio as stylistic choice
- Aspect ratio political ecology

**Example Article Structure (Template):**

```
---
title: "[Topic Title in Advanced Terminology]"
slug: "kebab-case-from-title"
date: "2026-04-13"
author: "Cinematic Essays"
category: "Film Theory"
description: "[130-150 char description with keywords, no thin language]"
---

## [Section 1: The Foundational Crisis / Historical Context / Theoretical Grounding]

[Opening paragraph establishing the critical problem. Use active voice. Cite film theorists by name.]

[Film example #1 with specific shot analysis. Use precise visual terminology.]

[Theoretical framework explanation. Cite: Deleuze, Chion, Genette, Mulvey, or contemporary theorists]

## [Section 2: Contemporary Case Studies / Formal Analysis]

[Analyze 2-3 recent films through the lens established in Section 1.]

[Specific shot descriptions using film terminology.]

[How this section advances the argument.]

## [Section 3: Production/Technical Implications / Methodology]

[How does this theory affect filmmaking practice?]

[Cite filmmakers, cinematographers, or recent interviews.]

[Technical or industrial context.]

## [Section 4: Spectatorial / Cognitive / Affective Dimensions]

[How do viewers experience this cinematically?]

[Reference psychology of cinema / phenomenology if relevant.]

[Why this matters for cinematic literacy.]

## [Concluding: Synthesis & Implications for 2026 Cinema]

[Synthesize insights from all sections.]

[What does this mean for contemporary filmmaking / criticism / spectatorship?]

[Future trajectory: where is cinema heading given this analysis?]
```

**Film Theory Depth Checklist (Every Article):**
- ✓ Cite 2-3 film theorists by name and concept
- ✓ Reference 4-6 specific films with formal analysis
- ✓ Use 8-10 advanced cinematic terms correctly
- ✓ Include 1 historical precedent (classical cinema example)
- ✓ Include 1-2 contemporary examples (2024-2026)
- ✓ Avoid first-person casual language (maintain critical register)
- ✓ No AI-generated filler ("As we've discussed..." vague transitions)
- ✓ Specific shot analysis over generalizations

**SEO & AdSense Compliance:**
- ✓ Description under 160 chars, includes searchable keyword
- ✓ No thin content language ("This is important", "It's fascinating")
- ✓ Every claim supported by film examples or theory
- ✓ Minimum 1,200 words (shows substantive effort)
- ✓ Section headers are descriptive, not clickbait
- ✓ Links integrated naturally (3-5 internal links per article)

**Stylistic Guardrails (Critical Register):**
- WRITE: "The apparatus theory framework, as articulated by Baudry, positions the cinema as an ideological apparatus..."
- NOT: "Movies are really cool because the camera does something neat..."
- WRITE: "This mise-en-scène strategy produces a specific spectatorial position..."
- NOT: "The scene looks really great and the viewer feels engaged..."
- WRITE: "The temporal montage structure, following Eisenstein's principles, creates dialectical meaning..."
- NOT: "The editing is fast and exciting."
