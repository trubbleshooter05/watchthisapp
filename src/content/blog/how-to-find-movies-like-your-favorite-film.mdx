## The problem with "movies like X" lists

Search **movies like Parasite** or **movies like Fight Club** and you will see the same recycled titles across a dozen blogs. Most lists optimize for SEO, not for *why* you liked the original film.

A better approach: decompose what you loved, then hunt for films that share that *vector* — not just the same genre tag on a database.

## Step 1: Name what actually stuck

Ask three questions after the credits roll:

1. **Mood** — Did you want tension, catharsis, absurdity, or melancholy?
2. **Mechanic** — Was it the twist, the social metaphor, the action grammar, or the slow burn?
3. **Aftertaste** — Did you want to rewatch immediately, argue about it, or sit quietly?

Write one sentence: *"I loved [Film] because ___."* That sentence is your filter.

## Step 2: Ignore lazy genre matching

Horror fans who loved *Get Out* do not necessarily want jump-scare slashers — they may want **social thriller** DNA. Sci-fi fans who loved *Interstellar* may reject space operas that lack emotional core.

Genre labels on streaming apps are distribution categories, not taste maps.

If you have compared three guides and still feel stuck, pick the film whose **opening mood** matches your night — not the one with the highest IMDb score. Recommendation quality is subjective; the goal is a satisfying next watch, not a perfect taxonomy.
